### PHASE 2: OPERATIONAL SAFETY ANALYSIS (The 3 Stages of Risk)

To ensure an "Apotheosical" standard of safety, we evaluate the bacteria during the three phases of the material's life:

#### 1. During Manufacture (The Growth Phase)
*   **The Process:** Bacteria are active, consuming urea and calcium.
*   **The Byproduct:** The metabolic process (ureolysis) converts Urea ($CO(NH_2)_2$) into Ammonium ($NH_4^+$) and Carbonate ($CO_3^{2-}$).
*   **Risk:** **Ammonia Gas.** High concentrations of ammonia are respiratory irritants.
*   **Mitigation:** The manufacturing facility utilizes a **Closed-Loop Scrubber**. The ammonia gas is not vented; it is captured and bubbled through water to create Ammonium Hydroxide.
*   **EFE Benefit:** This byproduct is a high-grade **Nitrogen Fertilizer**. The "waste" from the brick factory becomes the "food" for the hemp/corn farms growing the substrate. Zero waste; Zero hazard.

#### 2. During Residency (The Living Phase)
*   **The State:** Once the block is calcified (hardened), the bacteria encase themselves in the very stone they created. They enter a state of **Cryptobiotic Dormancy** (endospore state) due to the lack of oxygen and nutrients deep within the block.
*   **Risk:** Contact or Inhalation.
*   **Assessment:** The bacteria are entombed in calcite. They are not free-floating spores. Even if one drilled into the wall and inhaled the dust:
    1.  The dust is primarily calcium carbonate (chalk) and cellulose.
    2.  The bacteria are non-pathogenic and would be cleared by the respiratory system like common soil dust.
*   **Enhancement:** The high alkalinity (pH 9-10) of the interior matrix during the calcification process creates a **Biostatic Environment**. This effectively sterilizes the core against *other* harmful pathogens (like black mold, *Stachybotrys chartarum*, or Legionella) that often plague damp concrete or drywall.

#### 3. During Repair (The Healing Phase)
*   **The Trigger:** A crack forms; moisture enters.
*   **The Activity:** Bacteria wake up, consume the moisture/nutrients, precipitate stone, and go back to sleep.
*   **Risk:** Exposure to active bacteria.
*   **Assessment:** The activity is microscopic and contained strictly within the fracture fissure. The bacteria require a specific calcium/urea nutrient source to remain active, which is embedded in the matrix, not present on human skin. They cannot colonize the resident.
