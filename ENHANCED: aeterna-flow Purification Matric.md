### PHASE 4: SYSTEMATIC EVALUATION & ENHANCEMENT (Recursive)

**Analyzing Iteration 3:**

**Functional:** Does it stop viruses?
*Analysis:* Bacteria are large (0.5 microns). Viruses are tiny (0.02 microns). Ceramics stop bacteria but pass viruses.
*Gap:* Viral vulnerability.
*Enhancement:* **Crystalline Nanocellulose (CNC) Membrane.** Derived from wood pulp or hemp hurds. When spun into a film, the fiber spacing is nanometric. It mechanically blocks viruses without the water waste of RO membranes.

**Safety:** Bacteria growth in filters?
*Analysis:* Wet carbon filters breed bacteria.
*Gap:* Re-contamination.
*Enhancement:* **Chitosan-Copper Infusion.** Chitosan (from fungi/insects) is naturally antimicrobial. Copper ions lyse cell walls. We infuse the filter media with these to create a "Kill Zone" surface.

**Durability:** Can the housing take pressure?
*Analysis:* Mycelium is soft.
*Gap:* Burst risk under household pressure (40-60 psi).
*Enhancement:* **Resin-Infused Compression.** The Mycelium-Hemp housing is compressed under heat with natural pine rosin and dammar gum. It creates a composite stronger than fiberglass. The inner surface is lined with a spun **Borosilicate Glass** or **Glazed Stoneware** sleeve to ensure water never touches the structural matrix.

**Purity:** What about Fluoride and Lead?
*Analysis:* Carbon reduces them but is not specific.
*Enhancement:* **Bone Char** is standard for fluoride but raises ethical/sourcing issues. **Activated Alumina** is mined.
*Optimized Selection:* **Clinoptilolite Zeolite.** A natural volcanic mineral with a cage-like structure that specifically exchanges ions to trap heavy metals (Lead, Arsenic) and radionuclides, and has high affinity for ammonia. For Fluoride, we utilize **Tulsi (Holy Basil) Stem Biochar**, which has shown specific high-adsorption capacity for fluoride in recent studies, or **Hydroxyapatite** synthesized from calcium-rich agricultural waste.
